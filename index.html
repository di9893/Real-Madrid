Real Madrid CF has probably achieved all a football club could. With a record 35 La Liga titles, 20 Copa del Rey trophies, a sensational 14 European Cups/Champions Leagues and 2 UEFA Cups, Real are second to none in a competition for the greatest football club of all time. While the club enjoys fierce rivalries with Barcelona (El Clásico) and Atlético Madrid (El Derbi Madrileño), Real has been the most supported club in the country for the majority of its history. Many of the best players in the world have played in the clubs white shirts, including legends like Alfredo Di Stéfano and Ferenc Puskás.

History Though the club was founded in 1902, it was originally named Madrid Football Club; it wasn't until 1920 that King Alfonso XIII bestowed them with the title of "real" – or "royal" in English. These early years were reasonably successful for Real, as evidenced by their four consecutive Copa del Rey victories from 1905 to 1908. By the outbreak of WWII, Real could boast two La Liga titles and seven Copa del Rey trophies.

The post-war period saw Santiago Bernabéu Yeste take over as the club's president, after having carried various functions at the club since joining its junior ranks in 1909. Faced with the difficult task of rebuilding the club, Bernabéu went on to construct a brand-new stadium – later renamed as the Santiago Bernabéu stadium – and sign a number of high-profile international players to compete at the European stage. With a bonafide dream team centered around the talents of Alfredo Di Stéfano, Ferenc Puskás and Francisco Gento, Real cruised to four La Ligas and one Copa del Rey by the end of the 60s. More impressively, they claimed the first five European Cups ever organized, handily destroying any opposition in their path from 1956 to 1960. This legendary crew is still considered by many to be the greatest football team ever assembled.

The two decades that followed were as glorious for the club's growing army of supporters. In addition to cleaning up the domestic competitions by claiming an unprecedented 14 La Ligas and 5 Copa del Reys, the year of 1966 saw Real add another European Cup to their rich collection by defeating Partizan Belgrade 2-1 in the final. The decade was closed out on a sour note, however, with Santiago Bernabéu Yeste passing away in 1978.

Jude Bellingham has quickly become one of the brightest stars at Real Madrid since joining the club in 2023. Bellingham’s ability to control the game, break down opposition defenses, and contribute with crucial goals has already earned him a special place in the hearts of fans. And last but not least the Goat of football Cristiano Ronaldo’s,time at Real Madrid (2009–2018) is widely regarded as one of the most remarkable eras in the club's history. Joining from Manchester United for a then-world record transfer fee, Ronaldo quickly became the face of the club and a global football icon. Over the course of his nine-year spell, he broke numerous records, including becoming Real Madrid's all-time top scorer with 450 goals in 438 appearances. His exceptional ability to score, whether with his head, feet, or free kicks, revolutionized the club's attacking play. Ronaldo led the team to an era of unparalleled success, winning four Champions League titles and two La Liga trophies, along with countless individual awards, including five Ballon d'Ors. His relentless drive, professionalism, and leadership were key in solidifying his legacy as one of the greatest players to ever wear the white shirt of Real Madrid.
